The 2024 Design Pay Asia survey was conducted over an eight-week period from October to November 2024. The survey was distributed through design communities, social media channels, professional networks, and direct outreach to design leaders across Southeast Asia. A total of 1,797 valid responses were collected after data cleaning, which removed incomplete submissions, duplicate entries, and responses failing consistency checks.

The survey instrument covered compensation (salary, bonuses, equity), demographics, career history, negotiation behaviour, work preferences, and perceptions of design's organisational value. All compensation data was self-reported and converted to annualised figures in local currency. Where possible, outlier responses were verified or excluded based on statistical thresholds.

Several biases should be noted when interpreting results. The sample skews toward Indonesia (79.0% of respondents), which may over-represent Indonesian market conditions in aggregate statistics. The survey relied on voluntary participation, introducing self-selection bias — designers with stronger opinions or more extreme compensation experiences may be over-represented. Additionally, the digital distribution channels may under-represent designers without consistent internet access or those outside major design community networks.

Some cohorts in this report are too small to publish without identifying individuals, so we hold those figures back. See [data safety rules](/docs/data-safety) for the site's suppression and disclosure thresholds.

None of this cancels the findings, but it does mean market-specific breakdowns should carry more weight than the regional aggregate whenever the two disagree.
