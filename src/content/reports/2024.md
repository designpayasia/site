  body: |-
    The 2024 Design Pay Asia (DPA) Report presents findings from a survey of 1,797 design professionals across five Southeast Asian markets: Indonesia, Singapore, Malaysia, Vietnam, and Thailand. Conducted between October and November 2024, this is the most comprehensive compensation and career study for the region's design community to date.

    The survey reveals a young, dynamic workforce with 93.9% of respondents aged 35 or under. Indonesia dominates the respondent pool at 79.0%, reflecting both the size of its design community and our outreach efforts. Singapore contributes 9.7% of respondents, followed by Malaysia (5.1%), Vietnam (2.9%), and Thailand (2.4%).

    Across the sample, 52.5% of designers negotiate their compensation and 70.3% reported career advancement of some kind in the past year. Pay, work models, and perceptions of fairness vary by market: 70.1% of Singapore designers work hybrid, while regional markets report more mixed arrangements.

    This report serves as a benchmark for design compensation, career development, and workplace practices across Southeast Asia. It aims to empower designers with data to advocate for fair pay and to help organisations build competitive, equitable design teams.

  body: |-
    The 2024 Design Pay Asia survey was conducted over an eight-week period from October to November 2024. The survey was distributed through design communities, social media channels, professional networks, and direct outreach to design leaders across Southeast Asia. A total of 1,797 valid responses were collected after data cleaning, which removed incomplete submissions, duplicate entries, and responses failing consistency checks.

    The survey instrument covered compensation (salary, bonuses, equity), demographics, career history, negotiation behaviour, work preferences, and perceptions of design's organisational value.     Several biases should be noted when interpreting results. The sample skews toward Indonesia (79.0% of respondents), which may over-represent Indonesian market conditions in aggregate statistics. The survey relied on voluntary participation, introducing self-selection bias — designers with stronger opinions or more extreme compensation experiences may be over-represented. Additionally, the digital distribution channels may under-represent designers without consistent internet access or those outside major design community networks.

    Despite these limitations, the 2024 DPA dataset represents the most comprehensive view of design compensation and careers in Southeast Asia available to date. Readers are encouraged to consider market-specific breakdowns alongside aggregate findings and to interpret results within the context of their local market conditions.
