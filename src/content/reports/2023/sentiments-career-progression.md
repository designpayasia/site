## Career levelling progress

Over half of respondents (55.7%) reported making formal progress in job levelling in the past year. A substantial minority (30.7%) had not made progress, and 13.5% indicated the question was not applicable.

## Company support for growth

::chart{id="career-progression-support"}

Sentiment around company support for career growth averaged 4.54 out of 7. About half of designers felt their companies were supportive (scoring 5-6), but a significant number disagreed or felt ambivalent. This suggests uneven organisational investment in design career development.

## Learning and development

Designers showed moderate interest in exploring different disciplines and picking up new skills, averaging 4.34 out of 7. There was less appetite for switching career tracks entirely, with an average of 3.46 out of 7.

## Job search activity

Over half of respondents were open to switching jobs or changing companies in the coming year, averaging 4.34 out of 7 on job-seeking intent. Notably, 21% strongly agreed (rating 7) that they were actively looking.

*Sentiment findings reflect aggregate responses across 10 markets; 69.4% of respondents are Singapore-based.*
