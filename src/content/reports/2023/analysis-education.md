*The following data covers Singapore respondents only (N=223).*

## Education: experience trumps credentials

Those with no formal design education earn 23.6% more than formally design-trained peers, despite having slightly less design experience. This counterintuitive finding is explained by the fact that non-traditional designers tend to be older, highly educated career switchers who bring broader professional experience that employers value.

90.2% of designers without design education hold non-design tertiary qualifications. Bachelor's degree holders earn a median of S$82,400; Master's holders earn S$102,000. Tertiary education matters, but it does not need to be design-specific.

## Education type and outcomes

In-person certification correlates to 20% higher pay than having no design education, while bootcamp graduates trend at the lower end of median pay, though they are also the youngest group. Online certification sits between these extremes.

Larger companies and certain industries, particularly Consumer Products, appear more accommodating of non-traditional hires, suggesting that some employers value broader experience and transferable skills over design-specific credentials. Global companies pay up to 91.5% more for designers without formal design education, a premium explained by the career-switcher profile and the higher compensation bands at international employers.
